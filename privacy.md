## 2. Introduction
We are committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle your data when you use the Forge Hammer browser extension ("the Extension").

## 3. Data Processing Activities

### 3.1 Game Data and Chat Messages
The Extension processes data transmitted between your browser and the game servers to provide its functionality. This includes the analysis of chat messages.
- Purpose: To display them in the Extension.
- Storage: Chat message content is NOT stored. It is processed in real-time and immediately discarded after processing.
- Legal Basis: Processing is based on Article 6(1)(a) GDPR - Consent.

### 3.2 Mouse Movements and Clicks
The Extension detects mouse movements and click positions specifically within the game window to facilitate UI interaction.
- Storage: This data is stored only temporarily in your browser's volatile memory (RAM) for the duration of the ongoing browser session.
- Transmission: This data is NOT transmitted to any external server or third party. It is deleted immediately upon closing the browser tab or the browser itself.
- Legal Basis: Processing is based on Article 6(1)(b) GDPR - Performance of a Contract (necessary to provide the service).

## 4. Data Minimization and Local Processing
All data processing described above occurs locally on your device (client-side). No personal data is transferred to our servers or third-party servers, except where explicitly stated otherwise. We adhere to the principle of data minimization, collecting only what is strictly necessary for the Extension's function.

## 5. Cookies and Tracking
This Extension does not use third-party cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics services.

## 6. Your Rights Under GDPR
As a data subject, you have the following rights:
- Right of Access (Art. 15 GDPR): You may request confirmation as to whether we process your personal data.
- Right to Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR): You may request the correction of inaccurate data.
- Right to Erasure (Art. 17 GDPR): You may request the deletion of your data (note: since data is processed locally, you can delete the extension to remove all local data).
- Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18 GDPR).
- Right to Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR).
- Right to Object (Art. 21 GDPR): You may object to processing based on legitimate interest.
- Right to Withdraw Consent: If processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.
- Right to Lodge a Complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (e.g., the data protection authority in your country of residence).

To exercise these rights, please contact us at: forgehammer@outoftheline.de.

## 7. Data Security
We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk. Since data is processed locally, the primary security responsibility lies with the user's device security.
